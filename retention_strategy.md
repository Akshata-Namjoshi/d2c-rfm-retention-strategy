# Retention Strategy & Budget Allocation

## 1. Segmentation Summary
We have classified 2,400 customers into 7 distinct behavioral groups.

| Segment Name | Count | Avg Spend | Avg Recency | Description |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Champions | 107 | $6,009 | 8 Days | High spenders, very active. |
| Loyal Customers | 525 | $4,780 | 33 Days | Reliable, regular buyers. |
| High Maintenance | 103 | $6,644 | 32 Days | VIPs with >3 complaints. High Churn Risk. |
| At Risk | 228 | $4,441 | 207 Days | Former high-value users who stopped buying. |
| New Potential | 233 | $1,783 | 18 Days | Recent first-time buyers. |
| Hibernating | 536 | $1,203 | 217 Days | Low value, long gone. |
| Need Attention | 668 | $2,297 | 95 Days | Average users drifting away. |

## 2. Recommended Actions

### Priority 1: The "High Maintenance" Fire (103 Users)
   The Issue: These users spent $6.6k on average (highest of all groups!) but have ~3.4 support tickets each.
   Action: "White Glove" Service. The Support Team must manually review these 103 accounts.
   Business Impact: Saving just 10 of these users preserves ~$66,000 in revenue.

### Priority 2: The "At Risk" Win-Back (228 Users)
   The Issue: These users used to buy often (Avg Freq: 6.2) but haven't been seen in ~7 months.
   Action: Aggressive Win-Back Campaign. Offer a "Come Back" discount (25% OFF) valid for 48 hours.
   Goal: Reactivate them before they lapse into "Hibernating" forever.

### Priority 3: Champions (107 Users)
   The Issue: They are already buying.
   Action: Do NOT Discount. Offer "Early Access" to new products or a "Refer a Friend" bonus.
   Goal: Maintain engagement without eating into profit margins.

## 3. Budget Allocation ($5,000 Campaign Budget)
Since we cannot target everyone, we will allocate budget based on Potential ROI:

1.  50% ($2,500) -> "At Risk" Segment: High cost (discounts) but high reward (proven buyers).
2.  30% ($1,500) -> "New Potential": Small incentives ($5 off) to drive the crucial second purchase.
3.  20% ($1,000) -> "High Maintenance": Cost of sending apology gifts or expedited shipping to angry VIPs.
4.  0% -> "Hibernating": These 536 users are too low value ($1,203) to justify ad spend.
